About

In recent years, many roadside perception datasets have been created to accelerate the development of perception methods in urban environments, most of which predominantly concentrate on the intensive traffic in highway and urban road, or simply pin a fixed number of sensors to an intersection. However, perception tasks in a campus setting require different perspectives compared to their urban counterparts in the following aspects:

- (1) Pedestrians, Bicycles and Tricycles exhibit a higher prevalence in a campus setting than in urban traffic.

- (2) More occlusions happen due to crowded buildings, dense vegetation, slimmer paths and frequent corners in the campus environment.

- (3) while lane lines may present on a campus road, the absence of traffic signals and the scarcity of traffic regulations make it challenging to anticipate the behaviors of road users.

Although datasets exist for perception tasks in campus scenes, such as ShanghaiTech, NWPU, USyd and Campus3D, none of them have 3D information for targets from roadside view, not to mention the absence of consistent annotations to identify the same targets traversing across perception devices, which are critical for seamless and persistent object detection & tracking. Therefore, we present CORP, the first multi-modal Campus-Oriented Roadside Perception dataset, and anticipate that CORP will be a beneficial resource for the advancement of campus-oriented perception methods.

The campus area is divided into 3 regions based on different detection range, where a total number of 18 cameras and 9 LiDARs are involved in the dataset.
